A guided self-assessment for academy players. Used in the ninety-minute session, and yours to keep.

A note for parents

Read this first.

This workbook is for your child. It is part of the U18 Self-Assessment Session and it gives them a structured way to think about themselves before we meet.

It is built on the same frameworks the senior pros use. Mindset and behaviour, looked at separately. Strengths, and where strengths get overplayed under pressure. What gets the best out of them, and what drains it before they notice.

Spotlight, the formal psychometric, is licensed for adults only. The workbook is the U18 equivalent. Same intelligence. Different delivery. Built for the academy stage.

How to support your child with this.

Your job here is light. Hand it over. Let them work through it on their own, in their own time, somewhere quiet. Twenty to thirty minutes is enough. They can come back to it across a few sittings if they want.

There are no right answers and no wrong ones. The questions are designed to surface honest reflection, not to test anything. If your child wants to talk about a question with you, brilliant. If they want to keep their answers private until the session, that is also right.

What they write here belongs to them. It comes into the session with us. We will share back with you what your child is comfortable sharing, on the terms we agree together.

Confidentiality, in plain words

The workbook is your child's. The session is your child's. What is shared with you afterwards is agreed with your child first.

Two exceptions hold regardless. If there is a safeguarding concern, I will let you know and involve the right professionals. If the work moves beyond what I am trained to support, I will explain that and help you find the right specialist.

Tim Pattenden is the registered Safeguarding Lead at Pattenden Peak Performance, qualified to Level 3.

A note for you

This is for you.

You picked up the game because you loved it. Somewhere between then and now, the noise around it got louder. Coaches. Parents. Other players. The voice in your own head on the walk back from training.

This workbook is not another voice telling you what to do. It is a way for you to look at yourself the way the people who are good at this look at themselves. Honestly. Without flinching. Without performing.

There are no right answers. There is no scoring. Nothing here goes to your club, your coach, or anyone else. It comes into our session together and we work from it.

How to do it.

Find twenty or thirty minutes somewhere quiet. Not on the team bus. Not five minutes before training. Somewhere your phone is not in your hand, or rather, where the only thing it's doing is showing you this workbook.

Answer the way you would talk if you knew nobody was going to judge what you said. The more honest you are, the more useful the session is. That is the whole trade.

You can save and come back. Your answers stay on this device until you finish and download. Nothing is sent anywhere automatically.

The point is not to pass the workbook. The point is to know yourself well enough that you stop being surprised by what you do under pressure.
Part 01 of 07
Part 01

Your football right now.

Where you are. Not where you should be. Where you actually are.

Use a sentence or two for each. Don't worry about getting the wording right. Just write what comes.

Part 02

Mindset.

How you view it. Calm or alert, hopeful or careful, holding back or jumping in.

Your mindset is the lens you see the game through before you do anything. There are four mindset preferences and everyone has all four. The question is which ones you lead with, especially when the load comes on.

The questions below are not a test. They surface where you naturally sit. The session is where we make sense of the pattern.

Part 03

Behaviour.

How you do it. The way you actually show up, on and off the pitch.

Behaviour is how mindset turns into action. There are four behavioural preferences and everyone has all four. The question is which ones lead, and whether you can flex when the situation needs something different.

Same shape as before. No right answers. Just answer how you actually are, not how you'd like to be.

Part 04

Under pressure.

What changes about you when the load comes on. The bit that matters most.

Pressure changes everyone. The question is what changes in you, and whether you notice it before it costs you something.

Pressure can be a big game, a coach watching from a particular angle, a parent on the touchline, a run of bad form, a contract conversation coming up, a new manager, a teammate getting picked over you, a mistake on the first touch.

Tick the situations where you notice yourself changing the most.

More than one is fine. Most players have a few.

    When you are under that pressure, what shows up?

    Pick the lines that ring true. Tick as many as fit.

      Part 05

      Strengths in motion.

      What works for you, and where it sometimes works against you.

      Strengths and weaknesses are two sides of the same coin. The thing you are best at, used in the wrong moment, is the thing that costs you. Same skill. Different timing.

      A player who is brilliant at driving the tempo can wear teammates out. A player who reads the room beautifully can hesitate when a direct call is what's needed. Strengths overplayed are weaknesses in disguise.

      This part asks you to look at your strengths the way a senior player would. With pride and with honesty.

      Your top three strengths as a footballer.

      Technical, physical, mental, social. Whatever they are. In your own words.

      What people who know you well say about you.

      Two parents. A coach. A teammate. A friend who isn't in football. What is the line that comes up?

      Part 06

      Your engine.

      What gets the best out of you, and what drains it before you notice.

      Every player has an engine. The thing that fuels them when it is going well, and the thing that empties them out when it is not. Most players know one and not the other.

      This part is about both.

      Fuel.

      What gets the best out of you. Be specific.

      Drainers.

      What pulls the energy out of you, often without you noticing it happening.

      Recovery.

      What you do, or don't do, after a hard week or a bad game.

      Part 07

      What you want from the session.

      Three priorities. In your words. So we don't waste time.

      In ninety minutes we can do real work. We don't have to do everything. We do have to do the right things.

      Pick three priorities. They can be specific ("the way I freeze in the first ten minutes") or broad ("I don't know who I am if I don't make it"). Whatever is real for you.

      Anything else you want me to know before we meet.

      Doesn't have to be about football. Anything you'd want me to know going in. Sleep, school, mates, family, injuries, things on your mind.

      Before we meet

      What happens next.

      Bring your downloaded PDF to the session. We'll work through what you have written, and what you haven't.

      There are no marks for finishing every question. Some players come in having written ten lines. Some come in having written ten pages. Both work. The session adapts to what is in front of us.

      You will leave the session with a written Priority Action Plan. Three or four things, written in your language, that you can act on the same week. You will also have fourteen days of email access to me afterwards, and a fifteen-minute follow-up call at two weeks.

      The aim is not to fix you. There is nothing wrong with you. The aim is to help you know yourself well enough that you stop being surprised by what you do under pressure, and start having more choice about it.
